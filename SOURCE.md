## 4. INDONESIA MINING REGULATIONS

### Primary Laws
- **Law No. 4 of 2009** on Mineral and Coal Mining
- **Law No. 3 of 2020** (Amendment)
- **Law No. 6 of 2023** on Job Creations
- **MoEMR Regulation Nr. 26 of 2018** - Good Mining Principles and Supervision
- **MoEMR Decree Nr. 1827 of 2018** - Good Mining Practice Guidance

### Mining Safety Management System (SMKP)
- Wajib untuk semua mining companies
- Assessment and reporting requirements
- Diatur oleh MoEMR

### Environmental Monitoring (NEW - 2025)
**MOE Regulation No. 20 of 2025** - Standard Criteria for Environmental Damage to Land from Mining Operations (effective Oct 23, 2025)

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- **Semi-annual:** topsoil conditions, slope stability, erosion levels, landslides, surface-water flow, groundwater levels, mine openings, biodiversity
- **Annual:** ex-mining areas, vegetation coverage
- Report submission to MOE atau Governor

### Supervision Framework
Provincial/regional governments supervise environmental management melalui:
- Mining Inspector (MI)
- Environmental Inspector (EI)

## 10. KEY INSIGHTS FOR DECISION MAKING

### Why Compliance Monitoring might be better for SQE:

1. **Internal Customer Ready**
   - Sinar Mas Mining (GEMS) sudah punya ESG/compliance practices
   - 40+ financial services subsidiaries dengan compliance needs
   - Built-in pilot customers

2. **Regulatory Tailwind**
   - New MOE Regulation 2025 untuk mining environmental monitoring
   - SMKP requirements untuk mining safety
   - Increasing ESG disclosure requirements

3. **Less Competitive Market**
   - AI Compliance Monitoring: $1.8B market vs AI overall: $200B+
   - Easier to carve niche
   - Local/regional regulatory expertise as moat

4. **Technical Feasibility**
   - Lebih defined scope (SOP compliance vs general market prediction)
   - Data availability: internal process logs, SOPs
   - Lower complexity vs multi-source market data

5. **Clearer ROI**
   - Compliance fines are quantifiable
   - Risk mitigation has direct business value

### Why Market Intelligence might be better:

1. **Bigger TAM**
   - Much larger addressable market
   - More potential external clients

2. **Broader Application**
   - Can serve multiple industries
   - Cross-sell potential

3. **Competitive Against Giants**
   - Palantir, Crayon, Klue are expensive
   - Room for regional/SME-focused solution

### Risks for Each:

**Compliance Monitoring:**
- Heavy competition from established players (IBM, SAP, ServiceNow)
- Need deep regulatory expertise
- Integration complexity with existing systems

**Market Intelligence:**
- Requires massive, diverse data sources
- High accuracy expectations
- Competing with well-funded players (Palantir $2.8B revenue)
